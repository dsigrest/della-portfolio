Case Study 03 of 05 — Led design for sharing & embeds infrastructure

Sharing & Embeds

Senior Product Designer
Growth — Top of Funnel
Oct 2022 – May 2023
iOS, Android, Web

Challenge

Reddit content was already everywhere on the internet—but without attribution. Screenshots stripped context, URLs looked sketchy, and the platform had no recognizable off-platform presence. Seven-figure daily sharing volume, completely unoptimized.

What sharing looked like before — default iOS share sheet, raw URLs, no previews

Solution

I framed sharing as a three-stage system and owned the design strategy across all three. The goal: make proper sharing channels faster and more appealing than a screenshot, so attribution and brand travel with every piece of content that leaves the platform.

What sharing became — branded share sheet, rich previews, privacy controls

Research

I partnered with UXR on a longitudinal, foundational study that ran the length of this project—spanning social behavior on pseudonymous platforms, privacy perception, and sharing motivations across both Reddit users and non-users. The study shaped every stage of the framework.

Research overview — study scope, participant types (Redditors vs. non-users), key research questions

Testing Both Sides of the Share

We tested the existing sharing experience to identify breakdowns, then tested what we’d designed with the same participants to validate whether we’d addressed the actual problems. We also user-tested different preview formats with non-Redditors to understand what made sense, what didn’t, and what they’d expect after clicking.

Research methodology — before/after testing with same participants, non-Redditor preview comprehension tests

Foundational Questions

Our team’s core product questions shaped UXR’s study design—what motivates sharing on a pseudonymous platform, what inhibits it, and how do non-users perceive Reddit content when they encounter it in the wild? The answers built the behavioral model that informed every design decision in this project.

Key research findings — behavioral model showing sharing motivations and inhibitors

01 Streamline Sharing

Sharing had no dedicated infrastructure—no share button on posts or in the feed, no consistent controls across surfaces. This phase shipped iteratively: contextual entry points first, then the overflow standardization, then screenshot interception.

Section overview — before state of sharing entry points across the app

Surface Sharing Contextually

Sharing was buried in overflow menus—invisible at the moments users were most likely to want it. I mapped contextual triggers across the post detail page and feed, then surfaced controls directly at those points.

Share controls on PDP and feed — contextual placement of share entry points

Standardize Overflow Menus

Every overflow button in the app looked different—inconsistent across every surface. I designed a standardized overflow sheet with share controls at the top, then partnered with design systems and content design to establish a repeatable action hierarchy that held across surfaces with different tool sets.

Overflow menu standardization — before (inconsistent) vs. after (shared hierarchy with share controls at top)

Screenshot to Share

Users defaulted to screenshots because they were fast and certain. I designed a capture-and-share path that intercepted the screenshot gesture and channeled it into a branded, attributable flow. Instead of fighting the instinct, I built on top of it.

Screenshot to share — user takes a screenshot, gets prompted with a branded share card

02 Simplify Engagement

Wherever Reddit content appeared off-platform, it needed to be consistent, attributable, and enticing. I designed previews as a first introduction to the platform—a learning tool that established what Reddit is before anyone ever landed on it.

Cross-platform preview grid — same Reddit post rendered as previews on iMessage, WhatsApp, Twitter, LinkedIn

Aligning Off-Platform with On-Platform

I partnered with design systems to bring familiar UI into previews. Upvotes and comment counts served double duty—social proof to entice clicks, and education about how the platform works. Reddit’s logo anchored brand recognition for first-time viewers.

Preview component anatomy — callouts: upvotes (social proof + education), comment count (depth signal), logo (brand anchor)

Text Post Previews

Image and video posts had built-in thumbnails—text posts, the majority of Reddit content, had nothing. I worked with engineering to build a tool that generated digital screenshots of text as preview cards. We iterated on size, truncation, and cropping together—from rough desktop-page captures to crisp, legible cards that scaled for long and short form.

Text post preview iteration — early rough version (full desktop screenshot) vs. final crisp text card

Balancing Brand Expression and Systematic Scale

I worked with OrangeRed—Reddit’s external branding team—to identify the core brand components previews needed to carry. The tension: OrangeRed favored rich illustrative concepts; design systems pushed for scalable UI. The previews had to do both across six-plus platforms.

Brand expression spectrum — illustrative concepts alongside systematic UI components

03 Establish Trust

Streamlining flows and improving previews addressed the mechanics—but volume still wasn’t moving. The real friction wasn’t speed or aesthetics. On a pseudonymous platform, privacy was the core barrier to sharing.

Research insight — privacy as the core sharing barrier on a pseudonymous platform

Custom Share Sheet

I replaced the iOS default—a text list users scrolled through blindly—with a visual icon grid that surfaced each user’s most-used channels first. The grid reordered dynamically based on sharing history, with considerations for the cold-start default and the threshold for promoting a channel.

Custom share sheet v1 — visual grid with personalized channel order (before: text list / after: icon grid)

Share Preview and Privacy Controls

The first version shipped without a preview, and users still hesitated. I added a live preview of exactly what the recipient would see, plus an explicit privacy disclosure confirming the share wouldn’t expose the sender’s username. The preview became the trust mechanism—users could verify before committing.

Custom share sheet v2 — live preview + privacy disclosure added above the channel grid

Embedding Tools for Publishers

Publishers were already embedding Reddit content commercially—we weren’t giving them proper tools to do it well. I worked directly with publishers to build an embedding tool that made the attributed, well-formatted path easier than screenshotting.

Embed tool — publisher-facing interface for embedding Reddit content with proper attribution

The Result

Reddit went from zero sharing infrastructure to optimized, branded experiences across every major channel. The preview component system became the foundation for a public embed API, and the privacy-as-growth-lever framework shaped how growth teams across Reddit approached user control as a distribution strategy.

Public API Embed tools shipped for publishers
Phased Shipped iteratively across 8 months
