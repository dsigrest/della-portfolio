### Cursor: Developer Tools Awareness

**Added to AI / KLP case (same paragraph as above):**

## Voice Consistency

All v2 drafts use:
- **First person, present/past tense** — "I designed," "I learned," not "the team discovered"
- **Systems vocabulary** — "framework," "abstraction," "infrastructure," "governance," "leverage," "intervention points"
- **Proof through story, not claims** — Show the decision-making process, show the outcome, let the reader infer the quality
- **Specific over impressive** — "Cohort segmentation framework" > "advanced user segmentation"
- **Bold markers for scanning** — Key insight bolded, metrics bolded, section outcomes bolded

No hedging language ("arguably," "somewhat," "arguably"). No corporate jargon ("synergize," "stakeholder alignment," "best practices").

## Recruiter Panel Implications

From the recruiter evaluation:

- **Compression doesn't close hard requirement gaps**, but v2's gap-closing additions make the signal more visible without feeling bolted-on
- **Original versions had slight "protective ambiguity"** on hard gaps; v2 is more confident and more direct about what's being claimed
- **Compression speeds decision-making** (faster in both directions: advancement or rejection)
- **v2 is tighter and more scannable** — 5–6 minute reads with 90-second headline scans built in

**Recommendation from panel:** Original version marginally safer on hard gaps (ambiguity is protective), but v2 is stronger on clarity, scannability, and confidence signals. Since hard gaps require case-level edits (not version selection), v2's improved clarity outweighs the marginally increased gap exposure.
