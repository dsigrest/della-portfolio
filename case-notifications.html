Case Study 01 — Systems Thinking

Notifications & Inbox

Modernized Reddit's inbox after 6 years without dedicated design investment. Built a cohort-segmented engagement strategy that shaped the team's multi-quarter roadmap and freed critical navigation real estate for the platform redesign.

Senior Product Designer (Lead)
Notifications
2022 – 2024
iOS, Android, Web

Context

Reddit's inbox and notification systems hadn't had dedicated design investment in approximately six years. I was the first designer assigned to the Notifications team in that span. The surfaces — push notifications, email digests, the in-app inbox, and chat — were all managed independently with no unified strategy, no cohort segmentation, and no systematic approach to engagement.

This wasn't a visual refresh project. The challenge was to build the strategic foundation for how Reddit communicates with its users — from scratch, with no existing design vision to inherit.

Insight

Early in my exploration, I realized that the inbox wasn't one problem — it was five different problems for five different user cohorts. A casual lurker who visits Reddit once a week has fundamentally different notification needs than a power moderator managing 12 communities. A monolithic redesign would fail because it couldn't serve these competing needs simultaneously.

This reframe became the strategic backbone: instead of designing one inbox, I designed a segmented engagement system with different strategies for different user types.

System

I developed the Inbox 2.0 strategy built on three pillars, each mapped to specific user cohort needs:

  • Build Habits — For new and returning users: create consistent, valuable notification patterns that establish Reddit as a daily touchpoint
  • Enable Curation — For active users: give people meaningful control over what reaches them and how, reducing noise without reducing engagement
  • Create Focus — For power users and moderators: consolidate signal across channels so high-volume users can act efficiently
Inbox 2.0 strategy framework diagram — pending review

Simultaneously, I led the Unified Inbox initiative — merging notifications and chat into a single tab. This wasn't just a UX improvement; it freed a navigation slot that was critical for Reddit 2.0, the platform's broader redesign. The business case required cross-team negotiation with competing ownership interests across notifications, chat, and the platform team.

Experiments

With the strategic framework in place, I ran a series of focused experiments across push notifications, in-app inbox, and notification relevance:

Push notification optimization. Redesigned push notification content, timing, and targeting for new users. Tested variations in copy framing, send cadence, and deep-link destinations to find the right balance between engagement lift and notification fatigue.

Outcome

The Inbox 2.0 strategy and its three-pillar framework were adopted as the team's multi-quarter roadmap, giving the Notifications team a coherent vision for the first time. The Unified Inbox work freed a critical navigation slot for the Reddit 2.0 platform redesign. And the push notification experiments delivered measurable engagement lifts across multiple surfaces.

Beyond the metrics, the patterns I established — the cohort segmentation framework, the pillar-based strategy structure, the component library contributions — continued to guide the team's work after I transitioned to my next rotation.

Reflection

This project reinforced something I keep coming back to: the most impactful design work is often not about the interface. It's about building the strategic frame that makes good interface decisions possible. The cohort segmentation work — which produced no visible UI — was arguably the most valuable contribution, because it gave the team a shared language for making tradeoffs.
