## Context: Six Years Without Strategy

Reddit's inbox and notification systems had no dedicated design investment for approximately six years. **The surfaces — push notifications, email digests, the in-app inbox, and chat — were managed independently with no unified strategy or systematic engagement approach.**

I was assigned as the first designer to the Notifications team in that span. The challenge wasn't visual refresh; it was **building the strategic foundation for how Reddit communicates with users from scratch.**

[IMAGE: Inbox wireframe exploration and Figma notification design variants]

---

## The Insight: Five Problems, Not One

Early exploration revealed **the inbox wasn't one problem — it was five different problems for five different user cohorts.** A casual lurker visiting once weekly has fundamentally different notification needs than a power moderator managing 12 communities. A monolithic redesign would fail because it couldn't serve these competing needs simultaneously.

**This reframe became the strategic backbone:** instead of designing one inbox, I designed a segmented engagement system with strategies tailored to different user types.

---

## Three-Pillar System Design

I developed the **Inbox 2.0 strategy** built on three pillars, each mapped to specific user cohorts:

- **Build Habits** — New and returning users: create consistent, valuable notification patterns that establish Reddit as a daily touchpoint
- **Enable Curation** — Active users: give meaningful control over what reaches them, reducing noise without reducing engagement
- **Create Focus** — Power users and moderators: consolidate signal across channels for efficient action

[IMAGE: Inbox 2.0 strategy framework diagram]

### Platform-Level Abstraction

Designing this system required **thinking beyond individual features.** The 5-archetype framework became an API for how product teams across Reddit should communicate with users. Teams building new features could plug into the framework rather than inventing notification strategies from scratch.

### Unified Inbox Initiative

I also led the **Unified Inbox initiative** — merging notifications and chat into a single tab. This freed critical navigation real estate for Reddit 2.0, the platform's broader redesign. The business case required negotiating competing ownership interests across notifications, chat, and the platform team.

## Experiments and Results

### Push Notification Optimization
Redesigned content, timing, and targeting for new users. Tested copy framing, send cadence, and deep-link destinations to balance engagement lift against notification fatigue.

| Metric | Result |
|--------|--------|
| New user DAU | **+1.4%** |
| PN click-through rate | **+6.4%** |
| PN good visits | **+5.4%** |

### Subscription Notification Improvements
Optimized subscription-based notifications for reach, clicks, and visit quality.

| Metric | Result |
|--------|--------|
| PN good visits | **+1%** |
| Subscription good visits | **+2–3%** |

### Inbox Modernization
Redesigned the in-app inbox with updated visual hierarchy, clearer notification grouping, and improved action affordances. **The component patterns I established were adopted by the Design Systems team** into the shared library.

[IMAGE: Notification variants — grouped and action-oriented layouts, live UI, analytics dashboard]

---

## Outcome and Influence

The **Inbox 2.0 strategy and its three-pillar framework became the team's multi-quarter roadmap,** giving Notifications a coherent vision for the first time. The Unified Inbox work freed critical navigation space for platform redesign. Push notification experiments delivered measurable engagement lifts.

**Most importantly, the cohort segmentation framework continued to guide the team's work long after I transitioned,** providing a shared language for making tradeoffs.

---

## Reflection

The most impactful design work is often not about the interface — it's about building the strategic frame that makes good interface decisions possible. The cohort segmentation work, which produced no visible UI, was arguably the most valuable contribution because it gave the team a shared language for navigating competing user needs.
