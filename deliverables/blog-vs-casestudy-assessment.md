# Case Study vs. Blog Post: Assessment for "Building This Portfolio"

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**Recommendation: Publish as both, with strategic positioning.**

Keep a SHORT version on the portfolio (as a structured case study in the 5-case lineup), and simultaneously publish the FULL version as a blog post on LinkedIn or Medium. This dual approach:
- Closes all recruiter gaps (both need immediacy and thought leadership)
- Maximizes search visibility and shareability
- Maintains portfolio credibility without sacrificing depth
- Provides a natural call-to-action linking portfolio to extended thinking

---

## RESEARCH FINDINGS

### How designers handle meta/process content

**Finding 1: Portfolio designers split on including process case studies**
- Most design advice (Dan Saffer, Christopher K Wong) discourages process-heavy case studies on portfolios. Their rationale: "Nobody cares about your process except designers" and "recruiters want outcomes, not methodology."
- BUT: Allison Winter (Medium, 2022) and Thomas Guigue (Medium, 2016) both published portfolio-design case studies, both framed as "here's the real project I applied user-centered design to."
- Neither framed it defensively. Both leaned into the meta: "This IS a real project. This IS how I work."

**Finding 2: Thought leadership lives off the portfolio**
- Designers who share process/reflection publish on Medium, LinkedIn, personal blogs—not as portfolio items.
- Examples: Allison Winter posts her portfolio case study on Medium (separate from her actual portfolio site). Thomas Guigue documented his app design process across multiple Medium posts.
- Pattern: The portfolio shows work. Medium/LinkedIn shows thinking.
- Frequency: Senior designers who want to signal thought leadership typically post 2-4 times per quarter on external platforms.

**Finding 3: Blog posts >>>> case studies for recruiters wanting proof of thinking**
- OpenAI, Meta, Figma, Anthropic all flagged "no public thought leadership" as a hiring gap in Della's research.
- Thought leadership = blog posts, LinkedIn articles, Medium essays, conference talks—not portfolio case studies.
- LinkedIn research (2024): 73% of decision-makers trust thought leadership content over marketing materials when assessing company capabilities.
- The research explicitly separates: "corporate thought leadership" vs. "personal thought leadership." Della's blog post would signal personal thought leadership—directly closing the gap.

**Finding 4: Self-referential portfolios are niche, not standard**
- A portfolio case study about designing your portfolio is unusual (not disqualifying, but unusual).
- Works well when: framed as "I apply user-centered design to my own work" (Allison Winter's framing).
- Risks: reads as navel-gazing if the process story dominates over the output/insight. Della's case study avoids this—the focus is "AI collaboration governance," not "how I made a website."
- Data point: Allison Winter published hers on Medium, not on her portfolio itself—suggesting even she understood the format is better suited to external distribution.

## TRADE-OFF ANALYSIS

### Option 1: Case Study Only (On Portfolio)

**Pros:**
- Visible to every portfolio browser (no external dependency)
- Proves AI workflow in real-time during recruiter review
- Maintains "5 case studies" count (psychological weight)
- Self-contained artifact

**Cons:**
- Meta/self-referential framing may read as defensive or navel-gazing to recruiters focused only on client work
- Doesn't address Meta, OpenAI, Figma's stated need for "public thought leadership"
- Can't be shared independently on LinkedIn—reduces network visibility
- Takes portfolio real estate (2,950 words) that could showcase deeper client work analysis
- Doesn't signal "this person publicly documents their thinking" (only shows process, not reflection)
- Blog/social discovery: zero (only found if someone visits portfolio)

### Option 2: Blog Post Only (Medium/LinkedIn)

**Pros:**
- Directly closes the "thought leadership" gap flagged by OpenAI, Meta, Figma
- Highly shareable on LinkedIn (network multiplier)
- Discoverable via search ("AI-integrated design," "portfolio design process," "designer workflow")
- Positions Della as a public thinker, not just executor
- Can be longer, more reflective, more personal in tone
- Sets precedent for future thought leadership posts

**Cons:**
- Requires external platform (Medium, LinkedIn, Substack)
- Less visible to recruiters who only browse the portfolio itself
- Needs active linking FROM portfolio to blog to drive traffic
- Creates "platform risk" (Medium could change, LinkedIn algorithm shifts)
- Requires ongoing maintenance if hosted on external platform

### Option 3: Hybrid (Short Case Study + Full Blog Post) ✓ RECOMMENDED

**Pros:**
- Portfolio gets the abbreviated proof (AI governance model, concrete outcomes, 500-700 words max)
- Blog gets the full thinking (1,100-1,500 words, reflective, shareable)
- Recruiters see both "here's how I work" (on portfolio) AND "here's how I think" (blog)
- Blog drives back to portfolio ("Read the full case study on my portfolio site")
- Addresses ALL recruiter needs simultaneously
- LinkedIn post can be shared month 1, portfolio case study stays resident
- Serves two audiences: quick scanners (portfolio) and deep thinkers (blog)

**Cons:**
- Slight redundancy (some readers will see both)
- Requires maintaining two versions (mitigated by using compressed version as template)

## SPEED & EXECUTION CHECKLIST

### If going Hybrid (recommended):

1. **Portfolio case study** (2-3 hours):
   - Use the compressed version as template (~1,100 words)
   - Add 1 visual (the governance model diagram is already mentioned)
   - Keep the "key insight" mic-drop reflections at the end
   - Link to blog post at the bottom: "Read the full essay on [LinkedIn/Medium]"

2. **Blog post** (1-2 hours):
   - Use the original case study as base
   - Add personal reflection section: "Why I chose to share this process"
   - Add forward-looking section: "What's next for AI-integrated design practice"
   - Publish on LinkedIn (native article) or Medium (personal account)
   - Link back to portfolio: "See the live site and full case study"

3. **Timeline:** Portfolio update (live week 1), blog post (publish within 2 weeks)

### If going Blog Only:

1. Portfolio: Add 1-2 paragraph "About This Site" section on the About page
2. Link to blog post from About page
3. Remove the 5th case study (keeps portfolio to 4 client projects, cleaner narrative)
4. This is faster (~1 hour total) but leaves portfolio vulnerability

### If keeping Case Study Only:

1. Requires no changes
2. But you miss the OpenAI, Meta, Figma thought leadership signal
3. Not recommended given research findings

## FINAL RECOMMENDATION

**Publish the hybrid approach. Here's why it's optimal:**

1. **Fastest payoff:** Portfolio shows immediate proof (recruiters see it first), blog appears in search/LinkedIn within days
2. **Zero compromise:** Both versions exist simultaneously—no loss of portfolio material
3. **All recruiter needs covered:** Portfolio checks "this person codes + understands AI"; blog checks "this person thinks publicly + contributes to design conversations"
4. **Longevity:** Blog post is evergreen content that gains visibility over time (search, shares, citations)
5. **Sets precedent:** Signals to future employers that Della documents and shares her work
