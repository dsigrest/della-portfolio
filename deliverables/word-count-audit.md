## Critical Findings

### Finding 1: Systematic Under-Length Against Rubric Target

**All five compressed case studies fall 795–974 words short of the 1,800–2,200 word rubric target.**

- Notifications: 889 words (49% below target minimum)
- AI/KLP: 826 words (54% below minimum)
- Sharing: 982 words (45% below minimum)
- Subreddit: 899 words (50% below minimum)
- Portfolio: 1,005 words (44% below minimum)

**Why this matters:** The rubric target is based on hiring manager expectations and exemplar portfolio analysis (Simon Pan, Karolis Kosas, Buzz Usborne). Cases shorter than 1,800 words risk appearing surface-level, lacking the depth signal that senior design roles require.

**Root cause:** The compression strategy prioritized brevity over strategic completeness. The compression notes in each case study mention 40–63% reduction targets, but these targets may conflict with the rubric's depth expectations.

### Finding 2: Compression Backfired on Some Cases

The "compressed" versions are actually *longer* than originals in 4 of 5 cases:

- Notifications: +16.1% (766 → 889)
- Sharing: +74.1% (564 → 982) — expansion, not compression
- Subreddit: +39.8% (643 → 899)
- AI: +3.9% (795 → 826)
- Portfolio: −21.7% (1,284 → 1,005) — only true reduction

**Interpretation:** "Compression" meant restructuring for impact, not necessarily reducing word count. This created confusion: the markdown versions are richer and more detailed than the originals, making them *harder* to scan quickly, not easier.

### Finding 3: Scannability Exceeds Recruiter Attention Window

Four cases exceed the 90-second scan window (recruiter attention budget):

- Notifications: 113 sec (⚠)
- AI: 108 sec (⚠)
- Sharing: 96 sec (borderline)
- Subreddit: 108 sec (⚠)
- Portfolio: 84 sec (✓ acceptable)

**Strategic implication:** For recruiters to skim these cases in 90 seconds requires very aggressive information architecture. The 8–9 H2 sections per case aren't providing enough compression to fit a meaningful scan into the attention window.

### Finding 4: Metrics Sparsity Limits Scannability

Notifications case has 4 metrics (visible in tables). The other four cases have zero quantified outcomes.

**Impact:** Metrics are anchor points for recruiting-phase scanning. When a recruiter scans, they look for proof points: "What was the impact?" Lack of numbers forces them to read more prose to understand business value.

**Particularly weak:** AI/KLP, Sharing, Subreddit, and Building Portfolio cases provide no metrics anchors.

## Recommendations

### Priority 1: Expand Cases to 1,800–2,200 Words (Rubric Compliance)

**Action:** Add ~800–900 words per case by deepening these sections:

**Notifications (889 → 1,800+):**
- Expand "Experiments and Results" from 2 paragraphs to full section with subsections
- Add details on how cohort segmentation was validated (user research, A/B testing)
- Expand Platform-Level Abstraction (currently 1 paragraph) to show how framework scaled

**AI/KLP (826 → 1,800+):**
- Add quantified outcomes: Metric improvements, traffic consolidation, launch scale
- Expand "Prompt Design as Internal Tooling" — show workflow details, review gates, team adoption
- Add a Process section showing how governance model was piloted and refined

**Sharing (982 → 1,800+):**
- Replace metric table placeholders with actual numbers (or remove and shift to narrative)
- Expand "Three-Stage Approach" with more detail on each stage (currently 1 para each)
- Add validation: Did privacy controls actually increase sharing? Show the research.

**Subreddit (899 → 1,800+):**
- Expand "Community Lifecycle Framework" with more detail on each of the 5 stages
- Add process section: How was research synthesized? Which cross-org teams? What were findings?
- Quantify adoption: How many teams adopted the framework? Over how long?

**Portfolio (1,005 → 1,800+):**
- Deepen "What came out of it" section with specific proof points
- Add metrics: Portfolio completion time, recruiter engagement signals, job offers/interviews
- Expand AI governance model with workflow diagram or detailed walkthrough

**Implementation approach:**
- Add 150–200 words per section (Process, System, Outcome)
- Avoid repetition — each additional word should add *new* information
- Prioritize process details and quantified outcomes

### Priority 2: Add Metrics to Every Case

**Current state:** Only Notifications has metrics; others have zero quantified outcomes.

**Action:**
- **AI/KLP:** Add metrics on traffic consolidation, SEO impact, content coverage
- **Sharing:** Fill in pending metrics table (off-platform share volume, privacy adoption rate, traffic return)
- **Subreddit:** Quantify framework adoption ("adopted by X teams," "guided prioritization for 4+ quarters")
- **Portfolio:** Metrics on velocity (weeks vs. months), recruiter feedback signals

**Why:** Metrics are proof. They convert assertions ("strategy was valuable") into evidence ("strategy drove X% engagement lift"). This is especially critical for senior design roles, where impact quantification is expected.

### Priority 3: Improve Scan Structure for 90-Second Window

**Current issue:** 4 of 5 cases exceed 90-second scan time.

**Action:**
1. **Add visual hierarchy:** Ensure H2 headers are scannable in sequence without reading between them
2. **Bolding:** Increase bold emphasis on key phrases, metrics, and strategic insights (see Portfolio case model)
3. **Add images to Sharing and Subreddit cases** — visual breaks reduce scan fatigue and give attention anchors
4. **Reduce H2 count if possible:** Consider consolidating 9 H2s into 7–8 stronger ones for tighter scan

**Target:** Aim for 6–8 H2 sections per case (not 8–9) for 90-second scannable structure.

### Priority 4: Clarify "Compressed" vs. "Original" Intent

**Current confusion:** Markdown versions are sometimes longer and richer than HTML originals.

**Action:**
- Rename: "Compressed" implies brevity. These versions are actually "strategic restructuring" or "editorial revision."
- Or: Commit to true compression and cut another 30–40% from each case
- Document: Clarify whether the intent is brevity for recruiters (30–90 sec scan) or depth for hiring panel (full read)

**Current tension:** Depth and brevity are in tension. The portfolio can't simultaneously optimize for 90-second scan AND 1,800-word depth. A choice is required:

- **Path A (Recruiter-first):** True compression to 800–1,200 words with extremely aggressive H2/metrics anchors (current direction)
- **Path B (Depth-first):** Expand to 1,800–2,200 per rubric, accept 7–9 minute reads, optimize for full engagement (recommended)

**Recommendation:** Path B — expand to rubric target. Senior design roles expect depth signals. Brevity risks appearing unsophisticated.
