### 1. RAMP

**Previous score:** 7.0 | **New score:** 7.5 | **Delta:** +0.5

**Ramp's hard requirement:** Evidence of AI tools in design workflow

#### Scoring Rationale

Ramp values designer productivity and velocity. They look for practitioners who use AI as a collaborator, not just a buzzword. The compressed AI case study now presents this evidence **more clearly and concisely**.

#### Key Signals Found in Compressed Case Study

1. **"Prompt Design as Internal Tooling"** — This H2 reframe is excellent. It positions prompt design as a developer-facing infrastructure problem, exactly how Ramp thinks about design systems and tools.

#### Risk Assessment

**Risk: LOW.** Compression did not lose critical signals. In fact, tightening made the "internal tooling" framing clearer and more direct. Ramp recruiters will see the AI collaboration evidence they need.

**Verdict:** IMPROVED — Recommend for shipment with this company in mind.

## Compression Risk Assessment

### What Signals Were Strengthened

1. **Clarity of strategic reframes** — Tightening prose made core insights (flywheel, privacy-as-growth, trust-as-design-problem) stand out more clearly.

2. **Scannability for recruiters** — Reducing word count from 1,620–3,280 to 1,450–1,850 means recruiters can skim in 4–6 minutes instead of 8–13 minutes. This is **positive for conversion** — compressed cases are more likely to be fully read.

3. **Narrative focus** — Cutting filler forced stronger sentence structures and removed hedging language. This improved credibility and directness of voice.

4. **H2 specificity** — Restructuring headers from generic ("System," "Insight") to insight-driven ("Privacy as a Growth Lever," "Prompt Design as Internal Tooling") makes the strategic thinking immediately visible on scanning.

### Which Signals Were Weakened

1. **Process depth** — Some detail about experimentation methodology was cut to fit word limits. The notifications case, for example, no longer explains the A/B testing methodology in detail; it just shows results.

2. **Context/background** — Compression cut some contextual explanation of "why this problem mattered." Readers unfamiliar with Reddit might miss some stakes.

3. **Metrics completeness** — The sharing case study has metric placeholders instead of actual numbers. This is a **material gap** for Meta/growth-focused companies.

4. **Visual break-up** — Some image callouts were simplified during compression, though the prose still signals where visuals should go.

### Net Assessment

**Overall: POSITIVE compression impact.**

- **2 companies improved** (Ramp +0.5, Anthropic +0.5) due to clearer framing and better scannability.
- **2 companies declined slightly** (Meta -0.5, Figma -0.5) due to missing metrics and less explicit design systems framing. These are manageable gaps.
- **2 companies neutral** (Cursor, OpenAI) with no material signal loss.

**Critical action required:** Fill in the metric placeholders in the sharing case study before recruiting to Meta. Once those are completed, the portfolio will be **net positive** across all six companies.
