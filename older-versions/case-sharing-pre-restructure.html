Sharing & Embeds

Reddit had no sharing infrastructure—just the default iOS share sheet and unrecognizable URL strings. I framed sharing as a three-event system, owned the design strategy across all three stages, and discovered that on a pseudo-anonymous platform, privacy isn’t a non-issue—it’s the core issue. Serving 100M+ daily active users.

Senior Product Designer
Growth
Oct 2022 – May 2023
iOS, Android, Web

Reddit had no sharing infrastructure. The platform defaulted to iOS’s native share sheet—an unsorted list users scrolled through blindly—and shared links were long, unrecognizable URL strings with no preview. Recipients didn’t know what they’d get, so they didn’t click.

I framed sharing as a three-event system—distribution, engagement, and re-engagement—and owned the design strategy across all three stages. At the time, Reddit was approaching 100M daily active users. None of that scale was being captured off-platform.

Make It Easy to Share

I designed a custom share sheet to replace the iOS default—a visual grid that prioritized channels based on each user’s sharing history. WhatsApp-heavy sharers saw WhatsApp first. One tap, no scrolling, no reading through pages.

The first version shipped without a preview, and users still hesitated. They didn’t trust what their friends would see. So I added a live preview directly into the sheet—exactly what the recipient would get, with an explicit line confirming the share wouldn’t expose the sender’s profile. That preview was the trust mechanism. Users could confirm it didn’t look like spam before they hit send.

Earning surface placement was its own project. Feed, the post detail page, the overflow menu—these were contested surfaces mid-redesign, and every team wanted an on-ramp. I built the case for sharing as a top-of-funnel growth channel with data showing it drove both new user acquisition and brand awareness upstream—trust-building before someone ever landed on Reddit.

Make Shared Content Easy to Engage With

I led the design of a cross-platform preview component library—how Reddit content renders on iMessage, WhatsApp, Instagram, Twitter, LinkedIn, and in publisher embeds. I partnered with content design on copy, OrangeRed branding on visual identity, and Reddit Product Language on component architecture.

The design constraint: enough context to build trust, not enough to satisfy curiosity. A preview that showed the full post killed click-through. A bare URL killed trust. I landed on a format that surfaced the headline, a content thumbnail, and social proof—upvote count, comment count—with Reddit branding anchoring recognition for first-time viewers. Same principle I’d applied in notifications: show what’s behind the door, make users want to open it.

Cross-platform preview grid: show the same Reddit post rendered as a link preview on 4-6 different platforms (iMessage bubble, WhatsApp card, Twitter card, LinkedIn embed, publisher article embed). Demonstrate visual consistency across different platform constraints.

I designed these as a fixed component system rather than custom layouts per channel—each component rendered consistently across six-plus platforms with different display constraints. That architectural decision paid off twice: first in scaling to new channels without redesign, and later when the platform team used the same component library as the foundation for developer-facing embed tools—a public API that let third-party publishers natively render Reddit content in their own apps.

Help Users Feel Safe Sharing

Stages one and two shipped. Sharing got easier and looked better—but volume wasn’t moving the way we expected. I pushed for a research sprint, and what came back reframed the entire project.

Growth teams default to friction removal. The insight here was the inverse: friction was a symptom, not the problem. Reddit users maintain segmented identities across communities. Sharing a profile to someone who knows you in real life creates context collapse—your interest graph, your communities, your comment history all become visible. On a pseudo-anonymous platform, privacy isn’t a non-issue. It’s the core issue.

Context collapse diagram: show a Reddit user profile with multiple community memberships (subreddits) visible, with an arrow pointing to a real-life contact receiving a shared link. Illustrate how sharing exposes the full interest graph.

I led the privacy integration in the share sheet—controls surfaced at the moment of sharing, not buried in settings. I collaborated with the profile team to redesign privacy visibility on the profile itself—at a glance, users could see what others could see and update it. In post creation, I designed identity-selection flows—letting users choose who they posted as—and prototyped anonymous posting—experiments the creation team picked up and tested later that year.

Privacy controls across surfaces: 2-3 screens showing (1) privacy toggle in the share sheet, (2) profile visibility settings with what-others-can-see view, and (3) who-am-I-posting-as selector in the post creation flow. Show these as a connected system.

Once privacy was addressed at the source—on the profile, in creation, and in the share sheet—previously silent cohorts started sharing.

The Result

Reddit went from zero custom sharing infrastructure to branded, trustworthy previews on every major channel serving 100M+ daily active users. The component library I designed became the reference architecture for developer-facing embed tools—a public API now used by third-party publishers. The privacy-as-growth-lever framework—my team’s core contribution—shaped how subsequent growth initiatives across Reddit approached user control as a distribution strategy, not a constraint.
