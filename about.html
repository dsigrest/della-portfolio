About

Senior Product Designer — Engagement Systems, Growth, AI

I spent 4+ years at Reddit designing engagement systems — inbox, notifications, push, email, chat — and I was usually the first designer to touch them. Five consecutive team rotations as the first (or first-in-years) designer: building vision from scratch, aligning cross-functional leadership, and shipping scalable patterns that outlasted my tenure.

My work spanned growth experimentation (push notification lifts, sharing flywheels, community activation), platform foundations (design system adoption, unified messaging surfaces), and AI-forward product design (Reddit's first LLM-powered product, prompt design workflows, content governance).

I joined Reddit in 2021 as an L2 and promoted to Senior (L4) by 2023. My pattern: join a team that either didn't exist or hadn't had design investment in years, build the strategic foundation — user problem framing, vision, scalable components, cross-functional processes — then ship and move to somewhere harder.

What I'm exploring now

I'm currently building an AI product design evaluation framework — testing where LLMs accelerate engagement system design and codifying workflows I piloted at Reddit. This includes prompt design review processes, design principles for non-deterministic systems, and practical before/after comparisons of AI-assisted design workflows.

This portfolio site itself was designed and built collaboratively with AI, which I'm documenting as a case study in AI-native design process.

Notable: CPO Vision Work

Worked directly with Reddit's Head of Design and CPO to prototype the one-year product vision, auditing all team roadmaps and identifying strategic gaps across the organization. (Scope is limited here due to confidentiality, but happy to discuss in conversation.)

What I'm looking for

Senior or Staff Product Design roles focused on growth/activation/retention, engagement systems (notifications, inbox, messaging), platform foundations, or AI-forward product design. I'm most interested in organizations where the design challenge is systemic rather than surface-level — where the work is about building frameworks that scale, not just shipping features.
