About

I design systems that make complex products clearer, faster, and easier to use.

At Reddit, I spent nearly five years working across inbox, notifications, push, chat, growth, moderation, and AI — often as the first designer in ambiguous product spaces. My role was to turn complexity into structure: define the vision, align teams, and create systems that could scale.

Some of my most valuable work has lived beneath the interface: decision frameworks, reusable patterns, and prototypes that helped teams move faster and answer hard product questions with more confidence.

I move fluidly between strategy and craft, using visual prototypes, coded experiments, and systems thinking to answer the question at hand.

Right now, I'm especially interested in AI-native product experiences, design/developer workflows, and systems that shape user behavior at scale.
